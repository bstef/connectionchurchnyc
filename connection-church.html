⚡ Important Update — 2024

A New Chapter for
Connection Church NYC

In 2011, the McGhees and Mayberrys moved to Astoria, Queens to start Connection Church NYC with a desire to see people come to faith in Jesus and establish a healthy community.

In 2019, Connection Church sent out the Mayberrys and other families to Woodside, Queens to start Queens Church with a similar mission. And now, in 2024, Connection Church NYC is officially joining with Queens Church.

While this is the end of a season called Connection Church NYC, it is not the end of the movement and mission of its people.

We are profoundly grateful that the legacy and ministry of Connection Church continues through Queens Church and through all of those who have spread out around the world — and continue connecting people to Jesus and one another. If you feel like you’ve missed us, we welcome you to join us at Queens Church on Sundays at 10am or noon!

Our Story

A Movement
Thirteen Years in the Making

2011

The Beginning in Astoria

The McGhee and Mayberry families moved to Astoria, Queens with a singular vision: to plant Connection Church NYC where people could come to faith in Jesus and build authentic community — in one of NYC’s most vibrant and diverse neighborhoods.

2011 – 2019

Thirteen Years of Ministry

Through community events, summer intern programs, church partnerships, and years of faithful neighborhood presence, Connection Church NYC wove itself into the fabric of Astoria. Hundreds of people experienced faith, friendship, and belonging.

2019

Sending Out Queens Church

In a powerful act of missional generosity, the Mayberry family and several other Connection Church NYC families were sent to Woodside, Queens to plant Queens Church — carrying the same DNA and mission into a new neighborhood.

2024

Joining with Queens Church

Connection Church NYC officially merged with Queens Church, marking the end of one chapter and the beautiful continuation of its legacy. The mission goes on through every person who carries the heartbeat of Connection Church NYC into whatever comes next.

Church Partners

We Didn’t Do This Alone

From the beginning, Connection Church NYC was built on partnership — supported by a network of churches who sent people, finances, and prayer so that Queens could hear the Gospel.

Redeemer City to City

Redeemer NYC’s church planting network provided early support and resources for Connection Church NYC’s launch and growth in Astoria — part of its broader mission to multiply Gospel-centered churches across New York City.

Texas Baptists (MAP)

The Missionary Adoption Program partnered Texas Baptist churches with Connection Church NYC and Queens Church — providing financial support, volunteer teams, and ongoing prayer across both ministries.

Coastal Bend Fellowship

This Kingsville, TX church adopted the Mayberry family through MAP, sending volunteer teams to Queens and building a genuine two-way partnership that advanced the Kingdom on both ends.

What’s Next

The McGhees &
International Students, Inc.

After over a decade planting and leading Connection Church NYC, Daniel and Kari McGhee have stepped into new roles with International Students, Inc. (ISI) — a national ministry dedicated to sharing the love of Jesus with international students studying in the United States.

New York City is home to one of the largest concentrations of international students in the world. Daniel and Kari are now investing their lives into this extraordinary mission field right in their own city — on university and college campuses across NYC and Long Island.

They remain members of Queens Church, staying rooted in the community they helped build — while extending their reach to students from every corner of the globe.
